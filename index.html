What is Servant Leadership?

Servant leadership is a leadership philosophy in which the main goal of the leader is to serve. This is different from traditional leadership where the leader's main focus is the thriving of their company or organization. A servant leader shares power, puts the needs of the employees first, and helps people develop and perform as highly as possible. This approach can lead to higher engagement, more trust, and stronger relationships within teams.

According to Robert K. Greenleaf, who coined the term in his 1970 essay "The Servant as Leader," a servant leader focuses primarily on the growth and well-being of people and the communities to which they belong. This philosophy has been adopted by many organizations and is seen as a way to create a more inclusive and empowering work environment.

For more detailed information, you can visit the Greenleaf Center for Servant Leadership.

Servant Leadership at IMSA

At the Illinois Mathematics and Science Academy (IMSA), servant leadership is integral to fostering a community of empathy, innovation, and collaboration. IMSA encourages students to adopt servant leadership principles by prioritizing the needs of their peers and the broader community. This approach is evident in various student-led initiatives, such as the Student Leadership Council and Peer Tutoring programs, which emphasize service, empowerment, and shared responsibility.

By embracing servant leadership, IMSA students develop critical skills in empathy and collaboration, preparing them to become effective leaders in their future endeavors. The school's commitment to this leadership model not only enhances the educational experience but also contributes to a supportive and inclusive campus environment.

Applications of Servant Leadership

Student Council

Allows students to take on leadership roles and make decisions for the school, based on the needs of the students.

Board Positions

Allows students to lead certain groups, and help peers in various activities together as a group.

Big Sib/Little Sib Program

Connects sophomores with upperclassmen to help them attend to the needs of the sophomores and work together as a group.

Enhancing Servant Leadership at IMSA

One effective way to further implement servant leadership at IMSA is through a structured mentorship program. By pairing senior students with juniors, the program can foster a culture of empathy, listening, and community building. This approach not only helps new students acclimate to the school environment but also empowers senior students to develop leadership skills by serving as role models.

Research from the Harvard Business Review suggests that mentorship programs can significantly enhance leadership capabilities by encouraging leaders to teach and guide others. Additionally, the Forbes Human Resources Council highlights that servant leadership principles, such as empathy and active listening, are crucial in creating effective mentorship relationships.
